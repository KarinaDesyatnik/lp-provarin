SEXUAL DECLINE IN MEN

As men age, stress, lifestyle, and medical conditions can combine to initiate sexual decline.

Appearing between a man’s sexual prime and sexual dysfunction, sexual decline is a stage that affects most men over age 35.

SYMPTOMS OF SEXUAL DECLINE

Over 71 million men report symptoms related to sexual decline, including:

  • Diminished libido (desire for sex)
  • Softer, difficult-to-achieve erections
  • Reduced ability to maintain erections and sexual energy

WHAT CAUSES THE SYMPTOMS OF SEXUAL DECLINE?

As men age, a variety of lifestyle factors and medical conditions contribute to the breakdown of bio-drivers and trigger the symptoms of sexual decline.

Testosterone levels drop
35
A reduced libido, lower desire for sex, and worse overall sexual performance can all be caused by low levels of testosterone, which can go down 2-4% every year after the age of 35.
Blood flow decreases
40
By age 40, men produce up to 55% less nitric oxide than they did in their prime, which reduces blood flow, leading to softer erections that can be more difficult to achieve and maintain.
Metabolism slows
45
A sluggish and inefficient metabolism will reduce sexual stamina and the ability to maintain erections. After the age of 45, a man's metabolism can decline rapidly, causing a loss of energy and lower sexual endurance.
The breakdown of testosterone, blood flow, and metabolism has been linked to stress, smoking, poor diet, and lack of quality sleep. Certain medications and medical conditions (diabetes, high blood pressure, and cholesterol problems) may also be contributing factors for sexual decline and its accompanying symptoms.
